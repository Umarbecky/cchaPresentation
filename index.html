Headline insight

Readiness risk

This is based on a small sample of 10 respondents, so the data should be treated as directional user feedback and lessons learned rather than a statistical survey.

01 · Survey snapshot

Response overview

The survey captured structured ratings and open-text feedback across the full Phase 1 user experience.

How to read the results

  • Small sample: 10 responses means each response represents 10 percentage points when all answered.
  • Strong qualitative signal: repeated words such as training, system, team, information and upfront planning appear across multiple questions.
  • Action-focused: the strongest value is in turning lessons learned into Phase 2 readiness controls.

02 · Overall experience

Phase 1 experience was mixed to poor

Most respondents did not rate the Phase 1 experience positively, which points to readiness and adoption gaps rather than lack of platform potential.

80%

rated the overall experience as Poor or Fair

The safest message is not that Phase 1 failed, but that Phase 1 created a foundation while revealing issues that must be resolved before Phase 2 scales further.

“A more structured and transparent approach to readiness would have improved the go-live experience.”

04 · Training and support

Training is the strongest recurring theme

Feedback repeatedly calls for practical, team-specific, scenario-led training and refresher support.

Positive feedback is more cautious, but users identified benefits around case visibility, call summaries, dashboards and moving away from remote desktop.

06 · Pain points

The barriers are practical, not abstract

The most common concerns relate to training, navigation, information visibility, data, testing and internal workload.

07 · Delivery lessons

Delivery needs stronger readiness control

Users asked for more structured planning, clearer ownership, better session pacing, stronger requirements logging, and enough time for testing and re-testing.

Readiness

Use go-live readiness criteria covering training, testing, data, support routes, communications and user access.

Ownership

Agree a simple RACI so users know who owns decisions, testing, data queries and sign-off.

Workload

Work with Heads of Service upfront to protect time for workshops, testing and training.

Requirements

Keep one structured requirements and test basis, not several disconnected spreadsheets or channels.

Documentation

Create field mapping, data dictionary, reporting guidance and quick reference guides.

Scenario testing

Use real cases and team-specific scenarios, particularly where work is complex or varies significantly.

08 · Agile tools

Jira and Confluence were not clearly understood

Most responses were neutral or negative, and several comments show users did not know what the tools were or how they were expected to use them.

90%

were neutral, challenging or very challenging

Phase 2 needs a short “ways of working” onboarding session that explains Jira, Confluence, testing cycles, re-testing, decision logs and user responsibilities.

11 · Recommendations

What should happen next

The Phase 1 feedback should now be treated as a practical readiness checklist for Phase 2.

1

Run team-specific training

Use real scenarios from each team rather than generic system demonstrations.

2

Introduce a readiness gate

Do not move to go-live until training, testing, data, access and support readiness are confirmed.

3

Set up a clear hypercare model

Define where users go for support, who triages issues and how feedback is tracked.

4

Improve project communication

Use one agreed route for updates, decisions, actions, FAQs and known issues.

5

Explain Agile ways of working

Give users a simple introduction to Jira, Confluence, UAT, re-testing and acceptance.

6

Strengthen documentation

Create field mapping, data dictionary, reporting guidance and quick reference guides.

12 · Closing message

Phase 1 created the foundation. Phase 2 needs to create confidence.

The feedback shows that users can see potential in Salesforce, but adoption will depend on practical training, clearer delivery controls, better support and more realistic planning around business workloads.
